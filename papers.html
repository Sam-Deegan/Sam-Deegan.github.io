Bank Failures Still Matter: Evidence from a Natural Experiment

Deegan, S.

Job Market Paper 2025

This paper examines the real effects of bank failures using novel administrative data on credit relationships. I exploit the staggered failure of regional banks as a natural experiment to identify the causal impact on firm credit access, investment, and employment. Results show that firms exposed to failing banks experience significant reductions in credit, with larger effects for smaller firms and those with limited alternative financing options. These findings highlight the systemic importance of regional banking institutions.

Credit Supply Shocks and Firm Dynamics: Lessons from the European Sovereign Crisis

Deegan, S., O'Leary, P., & Murphy, K.

Revise and Resubmit Journal of Finance, 2025

We analyze how credit supply contractions during the sovereign debt crisis affected firm dynamics across Europe. Using matched bank-firm data from 2007-2014, we document significant heterogeneous effects by firm size, initial leverage, and geographic concentration. Small firms and those with high pre-crisis debt experienced larger reductions in credit availability, leading to decreased investment and employment growth.

Financial Fragility and Monetary Policy Effectiveness

Murphy, K., Deegan, S., & Walsh, T.

Published Economics Letters, 2024

This paper investigates whether financial fragility reduces the effectiveness of monetary policy transmission. Using data from 15 advanced economies over 1995-2023, we find that monetary policy shocks have substantially lower real effects during periods of elevated financial stress, with effects reversing during banking crises.

Deposits and Bank Lending: Evidence from Consumer Banking Data

Deegan, S. & Morrison, E.

Working Paper 2024

We examine the relationship between deposit dynamics and bank lending behavior using granular consumer banking data. We find that banks facing deposit outflows reduce lending more aggressively than those with stable deposits, and that this effect is amplified for banks with lower capital ratios and higher loan concentration.

The Transmission of Bank Stress in the Euro Area: A Network Analysis

Walsh, T., Deegan, S., & O'Leary, P.

Published Journal of Banking & Finance, 2023

Using network analysis methods, we trace the transmission of bank stress through interbank lending networks and the real economy. Our results show that stress in systemically important banks propagates more widely, with effects on credit availability for firms in multiple countries.
