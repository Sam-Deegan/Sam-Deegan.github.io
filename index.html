üìäMy Research

My research agenda focuses on understanding how shocks to bank balance sheets propagate through the financial system and affect credit availability to firms and households. I employ econometric and causal inference methods to identify the transmission mechanisms of financial distress. My job market paper, "Bank Failures Still Matter," provides novel evidence on the real economic costs of banking instability and informs policy discussions around financial regulation and crisis prevention.

Bank Failures Still Matter: Evidence from a Natural Experiment

This paper examines the real effects of bank failures using novel administrative data. I exploit the staggered failure of regional banks as a natural experiment to identify the impact on firm credit access and investment.

Credit Supply Shocks and Firm Dynamics: Lessons from the European Sovereign Crisis

Co-authored study analyzing how credit supply contractions during the sovereign debt crisis affected European firms. We find significant heterogeneous effects depending on firm size and pre-crisis leverage.
